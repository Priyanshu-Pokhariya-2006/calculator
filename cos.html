Cosine ($\cos \theta$)

Definition

**Cosine ($\cos \theta$)** of an angle $\theta$ in a **right triangle** is the ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse.

Formula: $\cos \theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$

On the **unit circle**, cosine is the **x-coordinate** of the point corresponding to the angle $\theta$.

Understanding Cosine

The cosine function describes the **horizontal component** of a radius vector rotating around the origin.

  • The **Adjacent side** is the side next to the angle $\theta$ (not the hypotenuse).
  • The **Hypotenuse** is the longest side, opposite the right angle.
  • The value $\cos \theta$ is the horizontal ratio (Adjacent/Hypotenuse).

Key Properties

  • **Range:** The value of $\cos \theta$ is always between $-1$ and $1$ (inclusive).
  • $\cos(0^\circ) = 1$ (Maximum value)
  • $\cos(180^\circ) = -1$ (Minimum value)
  • **Zeros:** $\cos \theta = 0$ at $90^\circ$ ($\pi/2$ radians) and $270^\circ$ ($3\pi/2$ radians).
  • **Period:** The function repeats every $360^\circ$ or $2\pi$ radians.
  • **Even Function:** $\cos(-\theta) = \cos(\theta)$.

Identities

  • **Pythagorean Identity:** $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$
  • **Cofunction Identity:** $\cos(90^\circ - \theta) = \sin \theta$
  • **Double Angle Formula:** $\cos(2\theta) = \cos^2\theta - \sin^2\theta = 2\cos^2\theta - 1 = 1 - 2\sin^2\theta$
  • **Angle Addition Formula:** $\cos(a + b) = \cos a \cos b - \sin a \sin b$

Examples

1. Standard Value: $\cos(60^\circ) = \frac{1}{2}$

2. Right Triangle: If the Adjacent side is $8$ and the Hypotenuse is $10$, then $\cos \theta = \frac{8}{10} = \frac{4}{5}$.

3. Double Angle: If $\sin \theta=\frac{3}{5}$, then $\cos \theta=\frac{4}{5}$ (from Pythagorean Identity). Therefore, $\cos(2\theta) = 2\cos^2\theta - 1 = 2(\frac{4}{5})^2 - 1 = 2(\frac{16}{25}) - 1 = \frac{32}{25} - \frac{25}{25} = \frac{7}{25}$.
