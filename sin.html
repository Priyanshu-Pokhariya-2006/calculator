Sine (sin θ)

Definition

The sine function is one of the fundamental trigonometric functions. In a right triangle, sine of an angle **θ** is defined as the ratio of the length of the side **opposite** to the angle to the length of the **hypotenuse**.

Formula: $\sin \theta = \text{opposite} / \text{hypotenuse}$

On the unit circle, sine is the **y-coordinate**.

Understanding Sine

Sine represents the vertical component of a point on a unit circle. When you have a right triangle:

  • The **opposite side** is the side directly across from angle θ
  • The **hypotenuse** is the longest side, opposite the right angle
  • sin θ gives you the ratio between these two sides

Key Properties

  • **Range:** $\sin \theta$ ranges from -1 to 1
  • **Maximum:** $\sin \theta = 1$ when $\theta = 90^\circ$ (or $\pi/2$ radians) + $360^\circ k$
  • **Minimum:** $\sin \theta = -1$ when $\theta = 270^\circ$ (or $3\pi/2$ radians) + $360^\circ k$
  • **Zero points:** $\sin \theta = 0$ when $\theta = 0^\circ, 180^\circ, 360^\circ$, etc. (or $0, \pi, 2\pi$ radians)
  • **Period:** The sine function repeats every $360^\circ$ (or $2\pi$ radians)

Applications

  • **Wave Motion:** Sine waves model oscillations, sound waves, and light waves
  • **Physics:** Used in simple harmonic motion, alternating current (AC) circuits
  • **Engineering:** Signal processing, vibration analysis
  • **Navigation:** Calculating heights and distances

Graph Characteristics of $\sin \theta$

  • **Shape:** Smooth, continuous wave called a sine wave.
  • **Amplitude:** 1 (maximum distance from the middle line).
  • **Period:** $360^\circ$ or $2\pi$ radians.
  • **Midline:** $y = 0$.
  • **Maximum Point:** $(90^\circ, 1)$ or $(\pi/2, 1)$.
  • **Minimum Point:** $(270^\circ, –1)$ or $(3\pi/2, –1)$.
  • **Zero Crossings:** $0^\circ, 180^\circ, 360^\circ$ $(0, \pi, 2\pi)$.
  • **Nature:** It is an **odd function** $\rightarrow \sin(-\theta) = -\sin(\theta)$.

Important Sine Identities

  • **Reciprocal:** $\sin \theta = 1 / \csc \theta$
  • **Pythagorean Identity:** $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$
  • **Co-function:** $\sin(90^\circ − \theta) = \cos \theta$
  • **Addition Formula:** $\sin(a + b) = \sin a \cos b + \cos a \sin b$
  • **Subtraction Formula:** $\sin(a − b) = \sin a \cos b − \cos a \sin b$
  • **Double Angle:** $\sin(2\theta) = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta$
  • **Half Angle:** $\sin(\theta/2) = \sqrt{((1 − \cos \theta) / 2)}$

Example Problems

Example 1: Find $\sin(30^\circ)$

Solution:
$\sin(30^\circ) = 1/2$

Example 2: If a right triangle has opposite = 5 and hypotenuse = 13, find $\sin \theta$.

Solution:
$\sin \theta = \text{opposite} / \text{hypotenuse} = 5/13$

Example 3: Find $\sin(2\theta)$ when $\sin \theta = 3/5$ and $\cos \theta = 4/5$.

Solution:
$\sin(2\theta) = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta = 2 \times (3/5) \times (4/5) = 24/25$
