Calculus

Definition

**Calculus** is the branch of mathematics that studies **change** and **motion** through derivatives and integrals.

It is widely used in physics, engineering, economics, and science to model systems where quantities are changing continuously.

Understanding Calculus

Calculus is primarily divided into two main areas:

  • **Differential Calculus:** Focuses on the **instantaneous rate of change** (the slope of a tangent line) using **Derivatives**.
  • **Integral Calculus:** Focuses on the **accumulation of quantities** (the area under a curve) using **Integrals**.
  • **Limits** form the fundamental basis of all calculus concepts.

Key Properties

  • **Power Rule (Derivative):** $\frac{d}{dx} (x^n) = n x^{n-1}$
  • **Power Rule (Integral):** $\int x^n dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n + 1} + C$ (for $n \neq -1$)
  • **Chain Rule:** Used for differentiating composite functions.
  • **Product Rule:** Used for differentiating the product of two functions.
  • **Fundamental Theorem of Calculus:** Links differentiation and integration, showing they are inverse processes.

Identities

  • $\frac{d}{dx} (\sin x) = \cos x$
  • $\frac{d}{dx} (\cos x) = -\sin x$
  • $\frac{d}{dx} (e^x) = e^x$
  • $\frac{d}{dx} (\ln x) = \frac{1}{x}$
  • $\int \frac{1}{x} dx = \ln |x| + C$

Examples

1. Derivative: $\frac{d}{dx} (x^2) = 2x$ (Using Power Rule)

2. Integral: $\int 3x dx = 3 \int x^1 dx = 3 \frac{x^{1+1}}{1+1} + C = \frac{3x^2}{2} + C$

3. Slope: Find the slope of $f(x)=x^3$ at $x=2$. The derivative is $f'(x)=3x^2$. The slope at $x=2$ is $f'(2) = 3(2)^2 = 12$.
