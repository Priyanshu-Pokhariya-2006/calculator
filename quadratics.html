Quadratic Equations

Definition

A **quadratic equation** is a polynomial equation of **degree 2** (meaning the highest power of the variable is two).

General Form: $$ax^2 + bx + c = 0$$ where $a$, $b$, and $c$ are real numbers, and $a \ne 0$.

Its graph is a U-shaped curve called a **parabola** that opens upward or downward. [Image of a parabola graph]

Understanding Quadratics

Quadratic equations are used to model parabolic motion (like trajectories of balls) and shapes. The behavior of the graph depends on the leading coefficient **$a$**:

  • If **$a > 0$** (positive) $\rightarrow$ the parabola opens **upward** (minimum value at the vertex).
  • If **$a < 0$** (negative) $\rightarrow$ the parabola opens **downward** (maximum value at the vertex).
  • The **Vertex** is the highest or lowest point of the parabola.

Key Properties

  • **Degree:** 2.
  • **Graph Shape:** Parabolic curve.
  • **Axis of Symmetry:** The vertical line that divides the parabola into two mirrored halves. $$x = \frac{-b}{2a}$$
  • **Vertex Form (Standard Form):** $y = a(x − h)^2 + k$ (where $(h, k)$ is the vertex).
  • **Discriminant ($\Delta$):** The part of the quadratic formula under the square root, which determines the nature of the roots. $$\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$$

Identities

  • **Quadratic Formula:** Used to find the roots (solutions, or $x$-intercepts) of the equation: $$x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$$
  • **Vertex Coordinates:** Can be found directly from the general form: $$(h, k) = \left(\frac{-b}{2a}, f\left(\frac{-b}{2a}\right)\right)$$
  • **Sum of Roots:** (When $x_1$ and $x_2$ are the roots) $$x_1 + x_2 = \frac{-b}{a}$$
  • **Product of Roots:** $$x_1 \cdot x_2 = \frac{c}{a}$$

Examples

1. Solving by Factoring:
Solve $x^2 − 5x + 6 = 0$. Factors are $(x-2)(x-3)=0$. Roots: $x=2$ and $x=3$.

2. Finding the Vertex:
Find the vertex of $y = x^2 − 4x + 1$ ($a=1, b=-4$). Axis of symmetry: $x = -(-4)/(2\cdot 1) = 4/2 = 2$. $y = (2)^2 - 4(2) + 1 = 4 - 8 + 1 = -3$. Vertex: $(2, -3)$

3. Using the Discriminant:
For $x^2 − 5x + 6 = 0$, $\Delta = (-5)^2 - 4(1)(6) = 25 - 24 = 1$. Since $\Delta > 0$, there are two distinct real roots.
