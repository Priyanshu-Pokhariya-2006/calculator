Linear Equations

Definition

A **linear equation** is an algebraic equation in which the highest **degree** (or power) of the variable(s) is **1**.

The graph of a linear equation is always a **straight line** [Image of a straight line on a Cartesian plane] .

General Form (One Variable): $ax + b = c$

General Form (Two Variables): $Ax + By = C$

Understanding

Linear equations are fundamental in math because they describe relationships with a **constant rate of change**.

  • They are defined by their straight-line graphs, where the **slope** is constant.
  • They can involve one variable (like $x$) or more variables (like $x$ and $y$).
  • They are widely used in fields like finance (simple interest), physics (distance/rate/time), and modeling real-world calculations.

Key Properties & Forms

  • **Degree:** 1 (i.e., no $x^2$ or $\sqrt{x}$ terms).
  • **Graph:** Always a straight line.
  • **Slope-Intercept Form:** $y = mx + b$
    • $m$: **Slope** (rate of change)
    • $b$: **y-intercept** (where the line crosses the y-axis)
  • **Standard Form:** $Ax + By = C$ (where A, B, and C are integers)
  • **Slope Formula:** $m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$ [Image of slope calculation on a graph] .

Identities & Formulas

  • **Point-Slope Form:** Used when a point $(x_1, y_1)$ and the slope $m$ are known: $$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$$
  • **Distance Formula:** Calculates the distance $d$ between two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$: $$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$$
  • **Midpoint Formula:** Calculates the coordinates of the midpoint $M$ between two points: $$M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \right)$$

Examples

1. Solving a One-Variable Linear Equation:
Solve $2x + 5 = 11$ $$2x = 11 - 5$$ $$2x = 6$$ $$x = 3$$

2. Finding the Slope:
Find the slope of the line passing through $(1, 4)$ and $(3, 10)$: $$m = \frac{10 - 4}{3 - 1} = \frac{6}{2} = 3$$

3. Writing the Equation (Slope-Intercept Form):
The line with slope $4$ and y-intercept $-2$ has the equation: $$y = 4x - 2$$
