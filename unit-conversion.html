Unit Conversion

Definition

Unit conversion is the process of converting a quantity expressed in one unit into another unit of the same dimension.

Units help measure physical quantities such as length, mass, time, and temperature.

Understanding Unit Conversion

Conversions depend on multiplication factors or ratios.

  • Metric units use powers of 10
  • Imperial units use fixed ratios
  • Temperature uses formulas

Key Properties

  • 1 km = 1000 m
  • 1 inch = 2.54 cm
  • 1 kg = 1000 g
  • 1 hour = 3600 seconds
  • 1 liter = 1000 mL

Identities

  • Celsius to Fahrenheit: F = (9/5)C + 32
  • Fahrenheit to Celsius: C = (5/9)(F − 32)
  • Kilometers to miles: mi = km × 0.621
  • Pounds to kilograms: kg = lb × 0.453

Examples

1. Convert 5 km to miles → 5 × 0.621 = 3.105 miles

2. Convert 72°F to °C → (5/9)(72 − 32) = 22.2°C

3. Convert 2500 mL to L → 2.5 L
