Tangent (tan θ)

Definition

Tangent of an angle **θ** is the ratio of the **opposite** side to the **adjacent** side in a right triangle.

Formula: tan θ = opposite / adjacent

On the unit circle, tan θ = sin θ / cos θ.

Understanding Tangent

Tangent describes the slope of the angle on the unit circle.

  • Undefined at 90° and 270° (or $\pi/2$, $3\pi/2$)
  • Linked to slopes and angles
  • Used widely in trigonometry and physics

Key Properties

  • tan θ is periodic with 180° ($\pi$)
  • tan θ = 0 at 0°, 180°, 360° (and multiples)
  • tan θ undefined at 90°, 270° (and odd multiples of $90^{\circ}$)
  • Range: $(-\infty, \infty)$

Identities

  • tan θ = sin θ / cos θ
  • $\sec^2 \theta = 1 + \tan^2 \theta$
  • $\tan(a + b) = (\tan a + \tan b)/(1 − \tan a \tan b)$
  • $\tan(2\theta) = 2 \tan \theta / (1 − \tan^2\theta)$
  • $\tan(-\theta) = -\tan \theta$ (odd function)

Examples

1. tan(45°) = 1

2. Opposite = 6, Adjacent = 8 → tan θ = 6/8 = 0.75

3. If $\sin \theta = 3/5$ and $\cos \theta = 4/5$ → $\tan \theta = (3/5) / (4/5) = 3/4$
