Angle Identities

Definition

Angle identities are **trigonometric formulas** that relate trigonometric functions of specific angles or combinations of angles.

They help **simplify expressions** and **solve trigonometric equations** by allowing substitution.

Understanding

Angle identities come from unit circle geometry and right triangle relationships.

  • Useful in simplifying trigonometric expressions
  • Used to evaluate trigonometric values for angles not on the unit circle (e.g., $15^\circ = 45^\circ - 30^\circ$)
  • Essential in calculus and physics

Key Properties

  • Relate angles through addition, subtraction, multiplication (double/half angle)
  • Fundamental to trigonometric simplification and proof
  • Illustrate the **symmetry** and **periodicity** in trigonometric behavior

Identities

Sum & Difference Formulas

  • $\sin(\alpha + \beta) = \sin\alpha \cos\beta + \cos\alpha \sin\beta$
  • $\cos(\alpha + \beta) = \cos\alpha \cos\beta − \sin\alpha \sin\beta$
  • $\tan(\alpha + \beta) = (\tan\alpha + \tan\beta) / (1 − \tan\alpha \tan\beta)$

Double Angle Formulas

  • $\sin(2\theta) = 2 \sin\theta \cos\theta$
  • $\cos(2\theta) = \cos^2\theta − \sin^2\theta$
  • $\tan(2\theta) = 2\tan\theta / (1 − \tan^2\theta)$

Examples

1. Using Sum Formula: $\sin(90^\circ)$

$\sin(30^\circ + 60^\circ) = \sin 30^\circ \cos 60^\circ + \cos 30^\circ \sin 60^\circ$
$= (1/2)(1/2) + (\sqrt{3}/2)(\sqrt{3}/2)$
$= 1/4 + 3/4 = 1$

2. Using Double Angle Formula: Find $\cos(2\theta)$ when $\sin\theta = 3/5$ and $\cos\theta = 4/5$

$\cos(2\theta) = \cos^2\theta − \sin^2\theta$
$= (4/5)^2 − (3/5)^2$
$= (16/25) − (9/25) = 7/25$

3. Undefined Case: $\tan(90^\circ)$

$\tan(45^\circ + 45^\circ) = (1 + 1)/(1 − 1) \rightarrow 2/0$, which is **undefined**
